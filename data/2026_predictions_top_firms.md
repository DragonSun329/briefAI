## Morgan Stanley

### European Equities 2026: Big Debates

**Key Insight:** European equities have outperformed US equities since US elections (in constant currency terms)

**Valuation Breakout:**
- For first time in 10 years, European equities broke the top of their discount range (December 31st)
- Historically, this signals discount narrowing over time
- Current like-for-like discount: 23% → could narrow to single digits

**Can't Compete on Headline Growth:**
- MS forecast: 4% European earnings growth
- US forecast: 17% earnings growth
- Europe won't outperform full year, but gap in performance will be narrower

**Bull Case for Europe:**
1. **AI Adoption** — Leading AI adopters (25% of index) showing strong earnings outperformance; trading at 27% discount to US equivalents
2. **M&A Rising** — Almost as sharply as in US
3. **Easing Competition Rules** — After decade of restrictions
4. **Corporate Re-leveraging** — Far from cycle peaks
5. **Savings & Investment Union** — Pensions reform, deeper capital markets

**Sector Calls:**
- **Long:** Banks (top of model), Defense (rearmament theme + seasonal Jan-Apr outperformance), Utilities (powering AI)
- **Short:** Autos, Chemicals, Luxury, Transport, Food & Beverage (old economy cyclicals, China exposure)

**Semi-Cap Space:**
- Double-digit global wafer fab equipment spend growth in 2026 AND 2027
- Memory supercycle — capacity constrained, long order visibility
- Humanoid robots: $300B global TAM by 2045

## PwC - Global Economy Watch

### Trade Becoming More Local
- Trade-weighted distance to key partners is shrinking for US and major European economies
- **US:** Average distance to import partners down 3% in 2023 — largest annual decline in 20+ years
- **UK:** Down 8% — largest decline in two decades
- **France, Italy:** Down 5% and 4% respectively

**Drivers:**
- Reduced exposure to Russia post-Ukraine invasion
- US and UK reducing trade with China
- Mexico now accounts for 15% of US imports (highest in 20+ years, larger than China for first time since 2002)
- Government incentives: $2T in US (IRA, Infrastructure Act, CHIPS Act), €750B+ in EU Recovery Fund

### Growth Outlook 2024-2025
- Global growth: 2.7% (2024)
- Eurozone slowdown bottomed out
- UK growth picking up
- US slowing sooner than expected
- China exports better than anticipated
- India continues to boom (friendshoring + supply chain diversification)
- **Peripheral Europe outperforming core** (Greece, Croatia receiving 13-16% of GDP in Recovery Fund)

### Demographic Shift
- Africa only continent with fertility rate >2.1 by end of decade
- Africa working-age population share higher than Europe/North America by late 2050s
- Advanced economies may look to African immigration to sustain growth

## BlackRock Investment Institute

### 2026 Global Outlook: "Pushing Limits"
*Released December 2, 2025*

**Core Thesis:** AI's buildout is dominated by a handful of companies spending at such scale it has macro impact. The challenge: will AI generate revenues matching the huge capital spending?

### Three Investment Themes

**1. Micro is Macro**
- AI capital spending so large that individual company decisions move the economy
- Overall revenues could justify the spend — but unclear how much accrues to the builders
- **Stay pro-risk, overweight U.S. stocks on AI theme**
- Great time for active investing (picking winners/losers)

**2. Leveraging Up**
- AI builders front-loading investment, revenues back-loaded
- Combined with highly indebted governments = more levered financial system
- Vulnerable to shocks like bond yield spikes
- **Private credit and infrastructure supporting this financing**
- **Tactically underweight long-term U.S. Treasuries**

**3. Diversification Mirage**
- With few mega forces driving markets, "diversification" could be big active bets
- Focus less on spreading risk, more on owning it deliberately
- Portfolios must be nimble with clear Plan B
- **Like idiosyncratic exposures in private markets and hedge funds**

### Key Macro Views

**Growth Breakout Now Conceivable**
- AI capital spending supporting growth in 2026
- Investment contribution to US growth = 3x historical average
- Capital-intensive boost to persist even as labor market cools
- US could potentially break out from long-term 2% trend for the first time

**Why AI is Different:**
- Not only an innovation itself, but potential to accelerate OTHER innovation
- Could learn from itself, improve, accelerate idea creation and scientific breakthroughs
- Tracking leading indicators: surge in new patents before benefits show in activity data

**AI Buildout Characteristics:**
- Unprecedented speed and scale
- Technology becoming capital-intensive (shift from capital-light)
- Higher leverage across the system
- Higher cost of capital
- Concentrated gains for now
- More room for alpha
- Need for big calls (no neutral stance, not even broad indexes)
